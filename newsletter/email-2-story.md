A confession: my favorite week in Watch Hill is the second week of October.

The summer people are gone. The Ocean House is at maybe a third capacity. You can park anywhere. You can walk into any restaurant. The light goes gold around 4:30pm and the water is still warm enough to wade into if you're brave. The carousel has closed for the season, the lighthouse museum has closed too, and somehow it's better that way — there's less to *do*, so you actually pay attention to where you are.

Ocean House drops their rates by something like 40-50% after Columbus Day. A room that's $1,200 in July is $650 in October. Same room. Same ocean. Same staff. The only difference is the temperature outside, and even that's mostly in the 60s.

Most travel guides won't tell you this because most travel guides are written by people who came once, in summer, and wrote down what they saw. The places I grew up around have a real shape across the year, and Watch Hill's shape is that the off-season is when locals actually enjoy it.

I'm not saying don't come in summer. Summer is great. I'm saying: if you have flexibility on dates, consider the second week of October. You'll thank me.

No link in this email, no ask, nothing to click. Just a thing I think more people should know.
